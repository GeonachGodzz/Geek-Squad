Operating System Comparison

Windows vs Linux

For this part of the case study we will be using Minecraft on Windows 10 and Linux Mint 22.1. We chose Minecraft because linux has issues with compatibility depending on the game.

Compatability

The Linux OS isnt as compatible with other games/developers like the Microsoft OS. This comes down to various reason such as:

  • Windows having a dominat market share
  • Linux having more distrubution making a one-size-fits-all support
  • Linux having a smaller and more technical user base, so companies are not incentivized to prioritize support or compatibility for the platform

Obeservation

To ensure we were only measuring the capabilities of each operating system, we kept the hardware specifications identical and configured Minecraft with the same video settings on both Windows and Linux.

What we will be looking for are:

  • Average FPS (Overall) – Shows general system performance and smoothness.

  • FPS While Loading Chunks (Moving) – Tests OS efficiency in handling disk I/O, memory, and CPU during heavy background tasks.

  • FPS While Not Loading Chunks (Not Moving) – Measures raw rendering and GPU performance in a controlled environment.

  • Multiplayer FPS – Reflects real-world network and background task handling under live interaction.

Summary

Windows consistently outpaces Linux in raw Minecraft performance—delivering higher average FPS (200–320 vs. 150–250) and superior idle FPS (340–355 vs. 200–250)—while both platforms perform similarly during chunk loading (~150–200 FPS) and in multiplayer (~40–74 FPS). This trend aligns with community benchmarks showing Windows’ driver optimization boosts peak frame rates, although Linux often delivers more stable performance during I/O-heavy actions due to lighter background overhead.
Community forums and Reddit discussions echo your findings: Linux often offers smoother and more consistent Minecraft performance during routine play, thanks to leaner background processes and efficient drivers.
